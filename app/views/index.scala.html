Supporting Developers, researchers, and everyone else who needs support

Have you ever worked on a project -- for school, work, or on your own -- and felt unmotivated? Have you ever felt afraid like your project was worthless, or you would get a bad grade? Have you ever needed help advice on the project? Have you needed support for it?

Many people go through these questions. And some people really do need support. That's what this site is for. You can post your project, and others can give you support by replying.

Consider people who develop software, especially those who make free software (freeware). These people compete against many others -- often millions -- to get people to notice their software. App developers compete against over 2 million apps on the App Store (“Number of available apps”), and web designers compete against over 1 billion websites (“Number of available websites”). It's very likely that nobody will see their software, and it will be be worthless. Yet a lot of developers make software -- even freeware -- for a living. They get their money -- to buy food, pay bills, and support their families -- from their software (Gambier 85). So it's very important for them to be successful (Kamp 49-51).
